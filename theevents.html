What is happening at the Hub?

Inspiring Space, vibrant Community and meaningful Content. The magic of the Hub is where these three elements connect in a hosted environment aimed to support your experience.

  • You work in a nurturing physical space, equipped with the tools you need, so you can focus on what matters.
  • You connect locally and globally with diverse individuals that share your intention to make a difference.
  • From everyday interactions to eye opening events, you get the inspiration and knowledge needed to take your idea to action and impact.

What are some of the events hosted at the Hub?

  •
    Cameron Sinclair, the founder of Architecture for Humanity (and advisor to the Obama administration), recently came to Hub Vienna to participate in an "ideas to action"-workshop for members with innovative ideas around green design and sustainable architecture.
    Activist Vandana Shiva presented in HUB Madrid the new foundation FABRICS FOR FREEDOM (FFF) and its main project EstaBolsaTieneTela in the Hub. FFF is an initiative that supports and promotes ecological fabric production and helps to market them, creating awareness of a responsible consumption.
    Coworking EU
    Back in November 2010 Hub Brussels brought you the Coworking EU conference co-organised by the Hub and member Jean-Yves Huwart. More than 150 attendees from 22 different countries came together to uncover the real potential of coworking featuring exciting presentations and inspiring debates.
  • In the spirit of ideas worth spreading, TED has created a program called TEDx. HUB Madrid hosted TEDxMadrid, where x=independently organized TED event. At our TEDxMadrid event, TEDTalks video and live speakers combined to spark deep discussion and connection in a small group.
    Slow Fish, Fast Fish, Smart Fish?
    In Hub Amsterdam, a dialogue took place between key stakeholders in the fishing industry to explore: What does smart development look like towards a healthy fish future? And what are leverage points towards a sustainable solution for developing countries and global corporations.
    How to organise sustainable meetings & events ?
    As a leading eco event space in Brussels we wanted to encourage others to start thinking about the environmental impact of their events & conferences. Thus we brought together leading event organisers to talk about how to run sustainable events and share best practices from throughout the industry.
  • Did you realize that co-creation isn’t only for work? In August Hub Brussels hosted Party 2.0; a collection of “micro-parties” that became one big amoeba of a party. Each member brought at least 1 ingredient (music, decorations, friends) and facilitated the co-creation of a community celebration.
    Joined by our friends, the Knowmads, we asked: How can we enable Social Innovation in the City of Amsterdam? How can this “generation E” contribute to a more generative economy? We found that The Hub can play a vital role in convening people, catalyzing new connections and supporting innovators to get started.
    The App Date is a place of connection, ideas, research and creativity on apps. Applications are turning it all: meet, exchange, investigate and bring to the table cutting-edge projects in the field of apps is what invites you on the last Monday of each month at HUB Madrid.
  • Author, Speaker and Business Model guru Alexander Osterwalder held a presentation on Business Model Innovation for Hub Milan Members. Main question: How can social ventures adopt and execute new business models? Every participants had the opportunity to experiment and try out various Models.
    Start-up Lab
    Allow entrepreneurs of innovative and social businesses to showcase their ideas, projects or enterprises to both a board of specialists and an audience of people interested on the issue. The goal is to test their business models, get feedbacks and contact players who might contribute to their businesses.
    Presentation of the Urban Matters initiative aiming for urban regeneration in Philippi, Cape Town: A diverse group including experts in sustainability architecture, creative youth development, employment creation and waste management looked opportunities they saw in the Philippi area and identified value adding solutions.
  • Hub School is an open learning space with 3 weeks of activities and workshops focusing on: entrepreneurship, innovation and consciousness. It is organized around a collaborative business model and gathers hundreds of people who actively work for the future they dream of.
    We hosted the first Startup Weekend in Milan. The participants attending the event decide what they want to tackle over the weekend and come out at the end with several projects. The winning startup is called "School Raising": a platform for school to raise money from the local community.
    For the opening of Hub Vienna, we had the pleasure to host the Social Business Tour with Prof. Muhammad Yunus. Next to having inspiring key note speakers, we also took the opportunity to sign an agreement with Grameen for the Hub Vienna to officially be run as a social business.
  • Hub Business Clinics
    In partnership with renowned universities, this event enable Hub members to make short presentations about their businesses to academics and specialists who provide Hub members with ideas, feedbacks and in some cases even new connections.
    Worldtalk
    High executives of global organizations are invited to speak about their CSR work. Click on the image above to see a short clip from the event.
    During the Milan Design Week 2011, The Hub Milan organized "Designing Innovation: ideas, works & story tales" a full week of inspirational lectures and workshops to discuss the future of sustainability and design. The Hub was open to the public the whole week, and people join us even at night for our sustainable dinners.
